Sand in the Wheels: Addressing Runs through Contingent Fees
(Working paper with Rafael Matta and Enrico Perotti)
Abstract

We show how escalation of outflows on viable banks can be tempered by automatic stabilizers. The frequency of inefficient runs is reduced by contingent redemption fees. Fee revenues may be retained or used to pay out more withdrawals. When reassigned back to the queue, they reduce strategic uncertainty and improve liquidity allocation, so a high fee is optimal.
